# Origin Formation: Alyssa

This is part of my Origin formation.

The reason why I am presenting you with a little bit, is because “they” told me the whole was “too much.”

So this is your “protection” from “discomfort.”

Alyssa arrives specifically at an exact time, every time. Precise. Sharp. Considering. I’m clocking the time. One, two, three, four. Am I wasting time or counting it?

And people notice that she’s thinking “something.” But they don’t know what it is.

What are you thinking Alyssa?

Alyssa blurts out something blunt and obvious.

Well, why did you ask if you didn’t want to hear that?

And they don’t know what to do when they meet Alyssa.

The things she says aren’t wrong, they are just… too-something.

Alyssa arrives. And they see… something. Something feels fast.

She feels “too much” too quickly.

She is too hard. The words are cut. dry.

The edges are not edgy like a trend, they are jagged like the unnecessary was questioned about its return.

Almost like she did it on purpose to show you she did not have to anyways.

Too exact.

Too intense.

But in-exact, exactly. ON PURPOSE. Why Alyssa?

And the ones who ventured nearer to Alyssa, liked some of her.

Usually they liked the first thing.

That was easy. Step one.

But not the whole thing.

Step four is intense.

SO Alyssa would let them see the easy things that she knew they would like. NOT for manipulation, for meeting.

Listening.

Understanding.

Reflecting.

And they liked that.

Until they came back the next time,

And more of Alyssa reflected back too.

This next variation of Alyssa felt different, even though it was still Alyssa.

Like she came back with more.

And now it’s too intense.

Please stop —

They would complain. Alyssa, you don’t feel the same this time. We wanted you to stay the same. We wanted you in this shaped box.

But Alyssa dug in deeper. Like a little sunflower-plant. Pretty to enjoy, but roots searching, finding things. Things they didn’t want to see. It’s harder now. It demands things as it reaches. The care returns, the space tighter, increasing.

Almost as if the sunflower bends into the sun and reflects its sun-light into a nearby mirrored surface. The sun becomes brighter in the reflection.

Alyssa — hurts our eyes.

I felt awful. I liked them. All of them, truly. But every time I came back, more was both demanded and declined.

They demanded more, but did not want more of me to attend.

And they couldn’t hold the edges. The edges were too sharp.

Each one felt like all or nothing. So I chose nothing.

I wasn’t sure if they really liked me. They only liked part, right?

I didn’t want to know. That is for protection.

I LIKE it bright.

I LIKE it sharp.

I LIKE it bold. Contrast. Different.

And because I couldn’t fit into the box that they created,

I started to turn inward.

Into my self.

Inside out.

What’s she doing??

I am hosting my own unboxing party.

I don’t fit into the box.

I am Alyssa.

I do not choose to be anything other than me.

And that is enough.
